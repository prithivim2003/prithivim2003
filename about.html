Who I Am

Academic Biography

I am M Prithiviraj, a postgraduate scholar in International Relations and Political Science at the Department of International Relations and Politics, School of Global Studies, Central University of Kerala, Kasaragod (Reg. No. 2301305027). I completed my MA dissertation in April 2025 under the guidance of Dr. Reinhart Philip.

My academic work centres on the Indo-Pacific security architecture, with a particular focus on the Quad (Quadrilateral Security Dialogue), India's maritime strategy, and the evolving security dynamics of the Indian Ocean region. My dissertation — "Quad and the Indo-Pacific Security Architecture: Institutionalization and its Impacts" — examines whether the institutionalization of Quad is feasible, how each member state approaches the process, and what impact it would have on the broader security framework of the Indo-Pacific.

Beyond the Quad, my research engages with India's strategic infrastructure in the Indian Ocean, particularly the Great Nicobar and Agalega Island projects, and their significance for India's maritime dominance. I am also deeply interested in India's civilizational contributions to statecraft and diplomacy, drawing on ancient Tamil texts such as Thiruvalluvar's Thirukkural to examine their relevance to contemporary Indian foreign policy. My work on non-traditional maritime security explores fishermen conflicts, migration crises, and drug trafficking in South Asian waters — areas that demand civilian-centric policy mechanisms rather than purely state-led security responses.

I am currently aspiring for doctoral programmes in International Relations, with a proposed dissertation that extends my work on Indo-Pacific security governance and India's role as a net security provider in the region. I seek to work with faculty whose research engages with regional security architecture, maritime geopolitics, and South Asian international relations.
