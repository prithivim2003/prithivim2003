Who I Am

Academic Biography

I am M Prithiviraj, a postgraduate scholar in International Relations and Political Science. My work centres on the academic and empirical study of international politics and security studies, where I strive to combine theoretical frameworks with historical empiricism, contemporary developments backed by data, and an objective focus to build analysis on international security.

My specific area of interest focuses on the comprehensive security dynamics of the Indo-Pacific region, specifically the Indian Ocean region. I have conducted extensive studies on the geopolitical developments in the region affecting its security architecture, and on developing mini-laterals such as the Quad, SQUAD, AUKUS, and ARF — examining how they strive to shape the security landscape of the region. I also focus on big power politics and maritime security conflicts between China and ASEAN members and other stakeholders. My primary objective in the security analysis of the Indo-Pacific is to analyse China's manifestation of influence in the region — its ambitions, manoeuvres, conflicts, defence build-up, and the arms race in the maritime Indo-Pacific — and how these developments collectively impact the comprehensive security dynamics of the region.

I have a strong conviction in India's approach to a rules-based order in the Indo-Pacific, and in its diplomacy to strengthen and empower regional developing partners for enhanced security and multidimensional cooperation. My dissertation examines the role of the Quad in the Indo-Pacific security architecture, how the Quad countries navigate the regional security dynamics, and China's counter-role within it. My ambition is to build a strong, data-backed, valid, and dynamic security analysis framework for the Indo-Pacific — and a roadmap for joint initiatives for long-term peace and development in the region by fostering a rules-based order.

I am also keenly interested in Indian national security and foreign-policy-led security initiatives.

on china
Research Focus

On China

I am an aspiring researcher on China's overall security, defence, and foreign policy studies. I strive to understand and decipher China's strategy and approaches to the 21st-century world order — its ambitions, intentions, and the risks posed to its immediate and more distant neighbours. My specific area of study on China is understanding its military and defence manifestations in the Indo-Pacific region and the nature of its relationships with regional partners.

I work on the US–China big power competition — the areas of contestation and the gaps between them, and the means by which both nations hedge on their respective strengths. Understanding this competition is central to any serious analysis of where the Indo-Pacific security order is heading.

on technology
Research Focus

On Technology

Lately I have been deeply interested in the technology security aspects of international relations. In the 21st-century world order, where technology has transformed human life in every dimension, that same technology can be wielded as leverage by nation states to hedge and compete — making it an expanding and consequential dimension of international security.

I explore the semiconductor prowess and computing capacities of nation states, which primarily drive the artificial intelligence landscape. The big power politics of semiconductors — where the United States holds much of the critical technology, East Asia serves as the powerhouse of manufacturing, and China is increasing its leverage through control of critical minerals and its computing ambitions to become the technological hegemon of the world — is a contest that will define the century. Technology has deeply penetrated the foreign policy, strategic partnerships, and defence and security architecture of nations, especially in the Indo-Pacific. The technology race will lead the 21st-century Indo-Pacific order.
